I think it's great and I appreciate the constructive feedback

This style of training is good for me. Once a week is not too much and not too little.

the bad ... nothing comes to mind ... except its only 6 weeks long

Question 5: What are we missing? Keep it Winlink Express usage or process specific, please.

A Live Exercise on a Saturday from multiple subgroups/locations

The flow of paper through the Winlink workstation could use some definition, IMO. From the moment a message arrives (as a verbal, "napkin," 213 Form, formal document, or as an incoming Winlink transmission); through its intermediate handling (whether transcribed to 213 verbatim, edited/paraphrased onto 213, scanned/photographed and attached to 213, printed out); then, if needed, its transmission (how to properly fill in the blanks); and finally its ultimate disposition and storage (hand-off to addressee, paper copies, electronic folders, 309s). And the numbering system, for new and related messages, verbal and Winlink, needs firming up.

Perhaps a bit more usage of the "Post Office" configuration so folks gain more understanding on how that works.

I think trouble shooting the Winlink connection procedure and computer settings would be a good idea as they are different with some radios.

What about non-Winlink Express software? I was specifically thinking of PAT Winlink for LINUX systems.

I feel like we've hit the topics that LOFD is expecting. Internals of Winlink Express would be interesting to a few, but not many, of us.

I think I mentioned before are actual usage during a call out. I am not sure people remember the How Tos that have been written and what will really happen. I submitted items like how do we do message numbers vs voice and how to integrate the 309? Also the winlink generated 309 misses the message number unless we establish a convention in message titles. All operational stuff for our ops manual.

After leading our training intro yesterday I do feel people are getting the message to be active on the net. Just want to make sure we educate on our processes too (sprinkled into the net and meetings).

The weekly Winlink Net has proven to be a success in terms of its availability, skills, and learning. Participation is still an issue. Always an issue handling that.

From my perspective, nothing is missing. I have come a long way from where I started for which I thank you and the Winlink team.

The foundations are good. The forms that we are using are the ones that I think we are most likely to use. I like the little quirks that each NCS puts into their cycle ? keeps things fresh and interesting even though most of the tasks at their base level are the same.

The main thing that is missing is the urgency that people will feel during a deployment, I think. The chaos, the noise?

The other think that we need to do more of is operate from the field. It would be great to have all of our operators be field-capable and practice that regularly.
